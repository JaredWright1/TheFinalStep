Chicken With Bamboo Shoots

This dish is an all-time favorite in chinese resturants. Here it is made by the authenic method, with the ingredients cut into small, neat slices, not tiny shreads

    Serves 4
  • 6-8 dried Chinese Mushrooms, soaked (page 63)
  • ½ lb skinless and boneless chicken breast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp lightly Beaten egg white
  • 2 tsps cornstarch mixed with 1 tb water
  • 2 Cups Vegatable Oil
  • 2 ½ Cups drained canned sliced bamboo shoots (see box, right)
  • 1 Scallion, cut into short sections
  • 2-3 slices peeled fresh ginger root
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp rice wine
  • 1 tb each light Soy and oyster sauce
  • A Few Drops sesame oil

Steps

  1. Squeeze dry the mushrooms and cut them into slices about the size of large postage stamps. Cut the chicken into thin slices about the same size as the mushrooms, and mix with a pinch of the salt, the egg white, and about half the cornstarch paste.
